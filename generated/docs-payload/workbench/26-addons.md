An **Add-on** is a custom-folder tool: a global extension that reserves a small declared area inside a project and follows a common SOP so that every Add-on is operated the same way. *Add-on* is the term the session glossary owns — the **model** (what an Add-on is, where it may write, the SOP it follows, its standard entry points, and the CLI/skill split) is defined in full at [session/15-addons.md](/session/addons/). This page records the **project-scoped application** of that model: what reserving a custom folder means at the workbench level, and how an Add-on integrates into the project's folder structure.

---

## The Folder Reservation

A custom folder reserves a small declared area inside a project's `.workbench/` descriptor. The rules that govern what may be claimed, and where bulk data goes instead, are in [session/15-addons.md](/session/addons/) (the "Tool Global, Data Project-Bound" section). At the project level this means: an Add-on's folder (`.memo/`, or whichever root it uses) is a **project-local consequence** of a globally-installed tool, not a standalone project folder. The taxonomy that distinguishes a custom folder from a convention is in [12-folders.md](/specification/folders/), which also carries the diagram of how registered folders, conventions, custom folders, and the wiki relate.

---

## The Memo System Is an Add-on Like the Others

The authoritative statement is in [session/15-addons.md](/session/addons/): the memo system is an Add-on like the others — a custom folder, not a conceptual special case. It is generated by `memo-init` and is the **recommended default** way of working, but the workbench *core* does not depend on it ([12-folders.md](/specification/folders/)).
